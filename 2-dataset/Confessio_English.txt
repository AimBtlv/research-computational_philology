

I Patrick, a sinner, most unlearned, and least of all the faithful, and most contemptible in the eyes of many, had as father Calpurnius, a deacon, the son of a certain Potitus, a presbyter, who belonged to the village of Bannavem Taburniae;

for he had a small estate nearby, where I was taken captive.

I was at that time nearly sixteen years of age.

For I did not know the true God and I was led into captivity in Ireland among so many thousands of men, according to our merits, because we departed from God and did not keep His commandments and were not obedient to our priests, who used to admonish us for our salvation:

and the Lord brought upon us the wrath of His indignation and scattered us among many nations even to the ends of the earth, where now my littleness is seen to be among strangers, and there the Lord opened the sense of my unbelief, so that, even if late, I might recall my sins and turn with my whole heart to the Lord my God, who regarded my humility and had mercy on my youth and ignorance and kept me before I knew Him and before I had wisdom or could distinguish between good and evil and strengthened me and comforted me as a father does a son.

Wherefore, however, I cannot be silent, nor indeed is it fitting, about such great benefits and such great grace which the Lord has deigned to bestow upon me in the land of my captivity;

because this is our recompense, that after correction and acknowledgement of God we should exalt and confess His wonders before every nation which is under the whole heaven.

Because there is no other God, nor was there ever before, nor will there be after these things, except God the Father unbegotten, without beginning, from whom is all beginning, holding all things, as we have learned;

and His son Jesus Christ, whom we testify was always with the Father, spiritually and ineffably begotten before the origin of the world before all beginning, and through Him were made the things visible and invisible, made man, having conquered death received into the heavens to the Father, and He gave to Him all power over every name of heavenly and earthly and infernal things, and every tongue shall confess to Him that Jesus Christ is Lord and God, whom we believe and expect His coming soon to be, judge of the living and of the dead, who shall render to each according to his deeds;

and He poured out upon us abundantly the Holy Spirit, the gift and pledge of immortality, who makes believers and the obedient to be sons of God and co-heirs of Christ:

whom we confess and adore as one God in the Trinity of the holy name.

For He Himself said through the prophet:

Call upon me in the day of your tribulation and I will deliver you and you shall glorify me. And again He says: But to reveal and confess the works of God is honourable.
