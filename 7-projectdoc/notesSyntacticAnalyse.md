## Sentence 1

> *Ego Patricius peccator rusticissimus et minimus omnium fidelium et contemptibilissimus apud plurimos patrem habui Calpornium diaconum filium quendam Potiti presbyteri, qui fuit uico bannauem taburniae;* 
1. Find the Sentence Skeleton
**What?**
- **root:** `habui`main finite verb, perf. ind. act.
- **nsubj:** `Ego`nominative first-person pronoun.
- **obj:** `patrem` (`habui`) direct accusative object, no preposition
- No **iobj**, **xcomp**, or **ccomp** in main clause
**Why?**
`habui` is the only fully inflected finite verb in the main clause. `Ego` is nominative and answers the question *who had*. `patrem` is accusative without a preposition and can be removed to make the sentence ungrammatical therefore `obj`, not `obl`.

**Notes?**
The subject `Ego` is explicit here, emphasizing Patrick's personal voice. All subsequent predicates in the text have an elided subject recoverable from this Ego.

9.  Verification

*What we do:* Verify the tree against the control criteria.

1. **The direction of the arrows is verified.** For prepositions and conjunctions, the head is a substantive word, not a function word. This is contrary to the intuition developed on Perseus.

2. **Coordination is built from the first conjunct.** Not from the final conjunction or comma.

3. **obj is used only for the direct object without a preposition.** All other indirect arguments and adjuncts are obl, with arg specification if necessary.

4. **Subrels are added where informative**, but are not used redundantly: a base label without subrel is acceptable if the specification is not critical to the analysis.
